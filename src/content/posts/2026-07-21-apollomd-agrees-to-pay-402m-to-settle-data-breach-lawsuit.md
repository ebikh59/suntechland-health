A recent data breach settlement in the United States has significant implications for Australian healthcare providers, highlighting the importance of robust cybersecurity measures to protect sensitive patient information. ApolloMD, a business associate providing integrated physician and practice management services, has agreed to pay $4.02 million to settle a class action lawsuit stemming from a ransomware attack in May 2025. The attack potentially exfiltrated files containing protected health information, including names, dates of birth, health information, and health insurance information, affecting approximately 626,540 individuals. This breach underscores the need for Australian healthcare organisations to prioritise cybersecurity, ensuring compliance with the Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles. The Notifiable Data Breaches scheme requires entities to notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner in the event of a data breach, which could have significant reputational and financial consequences. The Australian Cyber Security Centre's Essential Eight mitigation strategies and ACSC guidance provide a framework for healthcare providers to strengthen their cybersecurity posture and prevent similar breaches. Furthermore, the Security of Critical Infrastructure Act highlights the importance of protecting critical infrastructure, including healthcare services, from cyber threats. Australian healthcare providers must take proactive measures to prevent data breaches, including implementing robust access controls, regularly updating software, and conducting thorough risk assessments. The settlement serves as a reminder that the consequences of a data breach can be severe, and Australian healthcare organisations must be vigilant in their cybersecurity efforts to protect patient information and maintain trust. By prioritising cybersecurity and adhering to relevant regulations and guidelines, Australian healthcare providers can minimize the risk of data breaches and ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of sensitive patient information.

## What Australian Healthcare Organisations Should Do

- Implement multi-factor authentication to prevent unauthorised access to sensitive patient information
- Regularly update and patch software to prevent exploitation of known vulnerabilities
- Conduct thorough risk assessments to identify and mitigate potential cybersecurity threats
