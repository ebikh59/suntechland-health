A recent settlement in the United States has highlighted the importance of robust cybersecurity measures in the healthcare sector, with Lucent Health Solutions agreeing to pay up to $1.95 million to resolve a class action lawsuit stemming from a phishing attack in October 2023. The attack allowed a threat actor to access an email account containing the protected health information of approximately 37,000 individuals, including names, dates of birth, and health plan numbers. Although Lucent Health Solutions claimed the threat actor only had a 90-minute window to access the account and no evidence of data theft was found, the company notified affected individuals about the breach 15 months after it occurred. This delay and the alleged failure to implement reasonable cybersecurity measures led to a lawsuit, which has now been settled with class members eligible for reimbursement of losses and other benefits. For Australian healthcare providers, this settlement serves as a reminder of the need to prioritise cybersecurity and comply with the Privacy Act 1988 and Australian Privacy Principles, as well as the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme. The Australian Cyber Security Centre's Essential Eight guidelines and other ACSC guidance should be followed to prevent similar breaches. Furthermore, the Security of Critical Infrastructure Act highlights the importance of protecting critical infrastructure, including healthcare services, from cyber threats. Australian healthcare providers must ensure they have robust cybersecurity measures in place to prevent and respond to data breaches, and that they are transparent with individuals about any breaches that may occur. By doing so, they can mitigate the risk of legal action and reputational damage, while also maintaining the trust of their patients and the community. The settlement also underscores the need for healthcare providers to have incident response plans in place, to quickly respond to and contain data breaches, and to notify affected individuals in a timely manner. Ultimately, the settlement of the Lucent Health Solutions lawsuit serves as a reminder of the critical importance of cybersecurity in the healthcare sector, and the need for Australian healthcare providers to prioritise the protection of sensitive patient information.

## What Australian Healthcare Organisations Should Do

- Implement the ASD Essential Eight guidelines to prevent cyber threats, including phishing attacks
- Develop and regularly test incident response plans to quickly respond to and contain data breaches
- Provide regular cybersecurity awareness training to staff to prevent phishing attacks and other cyber threats
