A recent report from the Identity Theft Resource Center has revealed a surge in malicious insider incidents and mega data breaches, with at least 1,803 data compromises occurring in the first half of 2026. This trend is expected to continue, potentially resulting in over 3,600 data compromises by the end of the year. Australian healthcare providers should be aware of the potential risks, particularly in light of the Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles, which require organisations to take reasonable steps to protect personal information. The Notifiable Data Breaches scheme also mandates that entities notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner in the event of a data breach. While the report notes that there were relatively few mega data breaches in the healthcare sector, with only 7 healthcare data breaches requiring more than 1 million notices, the sheer volume of data breaches in the industry is still a concern. The report's findings highlight the importance of implementing robust cybersecurity measures, such as those outlined in the ASD Essential Eight, to mitigate the risk of data breaches. Australian healthcare providers should also be mindful of the Security of Critical Infrastructure Act and the need to protect sensitive information from unauthorised access. By prioritising cybersecurity and complying with relevant regulations, healthcare providers can reduce the risk of data breaches and protect patient information.

## What Australian Healthcare Organisations Should Do

- Implement multi-factor authentication to prevent unauthorised access to sensitive information
- Regularly update and patch software to prevent exploitation of known vulnerabilities
- Conduct regular security audits and risk assessments to identify and mitigate potential threats
