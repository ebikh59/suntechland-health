A recent report has highlighted a significant increase in healthcare data breaches, with 61 incidents affecting 500 or more individuals reported in May 2026, representing a 27.1% month-over-month increase. This trend is a concern for Australian healthcare providers, who must ensure compliance with the Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles. The Notifiable Data Breaches scheme requires entities to notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner in the event of a data breach. The report also noted that hacking incidents were the primary cause of data breaches, affecting over 266,000 individuals in one instance. Australian healthcare providers must prioritise cybersecurity measures, such as implementing the ASD Essential Eight, to mitigate the risk of data breaches. The Australian Cyber Security Centre provides guidance on cybersecurity best practices, which healthcare providers should adhere to in order to protect sensitive patient information. Furthermore, the Security of Critical Infrastructure Act highlights the importance of protecting critical infrastructure, including healthcare services, from cyber threats. As the report indicates, very large data breaches have decreased, but the number of incidents remains a concern, with at least 21,085,405 individuals affected by 319 data breaches from January 1, 2026, to May 31, 2026. Australian healthcare providers must remain vigilant and proactive in their approach to cybersecurity to prevent similar incidents. The ACSC guidance and the SOCI Act provide a framework for healthcare providers to follow in order to ensure the security of their systems and protect patient data. By prioritising cybersecurity and complying with relevant regulations, Australian healthcare providers can reduce the risk of data breaches and maintain the trust of their patients.

## What Australian Healthcare Organisations Should Do

- Implement multi-factor authentication to prevent unauthorised access to systems and data
- Regularly update and patch software to prevent exploitation of known vulnerabilities
- Conduct regular cybersecurity training and awareness programs for all staff to ensure they are aware of the risks and can identify potential threats
