A recent report by Sophos has revealed that compromised logins have become the most common entry point for ransomware attacks, with 79% of incidents traced back to an initial intrusion exploiting compromised identities and legitimate user logins. This surge in identity-based attacks has seen a corresponding drop in the exploitation of known security vulnerabilities, which was previously the most common root cause of ransomware incidents. The report found that malicious emails and phishing attacks were also common entry points, accounting for 26% and 24% of incidents respectively. Australian healthcare providers must take note of these findings, as they highlight the importance of robust cybersecurity measures, including multi-factor authentication and regular password updates, in preventing ransomware attacks. The Privacy Act 1988 and Australian Privacy Principles require organisations to take reasonable steps to protect personal information, and the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme mandates the notification of certain data breaches to affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner. The Australian Cyber Security Centre's Essential Eight provides a framework for organisations to protect themselves against cyber threats, and healthcare providers should prioritise the implementation of these measures to prevent ransomware attacks. Furthermore, the Security of Critical Infrastructure Act highlights the need for organisations to protect their critical infrastructure from cyber threats, and healthcare providers must take a proactive approach to cybersecurity to prevent attacks. By prioritising cybersecurity and implementing robust measures, Australian healthcare providers can reduce the risk of ransomware attacks and protect sensitive patient information.

## What Australian Healthcare Organisations Should Do

- Implement multi-factor authentication to prevent unauthorised access to systems and data
- Regularly update passwords and ensure that all employees use strong, unique passwords
- Conduct regular security audits and risk assessments to identify vulnerabilities and implement measures to mitigate them, in line with the ASD Essential Eight and ACSC guidance
