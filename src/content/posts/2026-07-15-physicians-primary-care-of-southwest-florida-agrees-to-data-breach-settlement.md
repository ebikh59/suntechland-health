A recent data breach settlement in the United States has significant implications for Australian healthcare providers, highlighting the importance of robust cybersecurity measures to protect sensitive patient information. Physicians Primary Care of Southwest Florida, a medical facility with multiple offices in Florida, was the victim of a targeted cyberattack in September 2024 that exposed patient data, including names, health information, and Social Security numbers. The breach, which affected 170,653 individuals, was reported to the US Department of Health and Human Services' Office for Civil Rights and sparked a class action lawsuit alleging that the breach could have been prevented if reasonable and appropriate security measures had been implemented. The settlement, which has received preliminary approval from the court, allows class members to submit a claim for reimbursement of documented, unreimbursed out-of-pocket losses due to the data breach up to a maximum of $5,000 per class member, as well as enroll in two years of medical monitoring services, including a $1 million identity theft insurance policy. This case serves as a reminder to Australian healthcare providers of their obligations under the Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of personal information, including sensitive health information. The Notifiable Data Breaches scheme also requires entities to notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner in the event of an eligible data breach. Australian healthcare providers must prioritise cybersecurity and implement robust measures to prevent and respond to data breaches, including those outlined in the ASD Essential Eight and ACSC guidance. By doing so, they can reduce the risk of a data breach and minimise the potential harm to individuals whose personal information is compromised. The Security of Critical Infrastructure Act 2018 also imposes obligations on certain entities to implement cybersecurity measures to protect critical infrastructure, including healthcare services. In light of this settlement, Australian healthcare providers should review their cybersecurity measures and ensure they are taking all necessary steps to protect sensitive patient information and comply with relevant laws and regulations.

## What Australian Healthcare Organisations Should Do

- Implement the ASD Essential Eight cybersecurity mitigation strategies to prevent malware and other cyber threats
- Regularly review and update cybersecurity policies and procedures to ensure compliance with the Australian Privacy Principles and the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme
- Conduct regular security audits and risk assessments to identify vulnerabilities and prioritise remediation efforts, in line with ACSC guidance and the Security of Critical Infrastructure Act 2018
