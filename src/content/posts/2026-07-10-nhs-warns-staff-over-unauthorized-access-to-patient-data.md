A stern warning has been issued to staff within Britain's health service, the NHS, regarding the unauthorized access of patient data, with potential penalties including jail time for those found guilty. The head of the NHS has deemed such actions as wholly unacceptable and a breach of patient trust, emphasizing that they are also against the law. This warning comes as part of a new awareness campaign and guidance for healthcare organizations on preventing, monitoring, and reporting unauthorized access. The campaign, prompted by several high-profile cases of NHS employees accessing patient records without legitimate reason, urges staff not to let curiosity compromise their careers. For Australian healthcare providers, this serves as a reminder of the importance of adhering to the Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles, particularly in ensuring the confidentiality and security of patient data. The Notifiable Data Breaches scheme also necessitates that entities notify individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner in cases of eligible data breaches, highlighting the need for robust data protection measures. Implementing technical controls such as multi-factor authentication, role-based access controls, and enforcing least-privilege policies, as suggested by the NHS guidance, aligns with the ASD Essential Eight and ACSC guidance, which are crucial for mitigating insider threats and external cyber risks. Australian healthcare organizations must prioritize these measures to safeguard patient data and maintain trust. The SOCI Act also plays a role in governing the use and disclosure of personal information, further emphasizing the need for vigilance. By adopting a proactive approach to data security, Australian healthcare providers can minimize the risk of unauthorized access and ensure compliance with regulatory requirements.

## What Australian Healthcare Organisations Should Do

- Implement multi-factor authentication for all users accessing patient data to prevent unauthorized access
- Enforce least-privilege policies and role-based access controls to limit data access to only those who need it for legitimate purposes
- Conduct regular audits and monitor for suspicious activity in real-time using newer electronic patient record systems to quickly identify and respond to potential data breaches
