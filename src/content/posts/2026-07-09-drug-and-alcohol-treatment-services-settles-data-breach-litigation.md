A recent data breach settlement in the United States has significant implications for Australian healthcare providers. Drug and Alcohol Treatment Services, a provider of drug and alcohol addiction services, has agreed to settle class action litigation stemming from a ransomware attack in October 2024. The attack resulted in the theft of personal and protected health information of employees and patients, including names, dates of birth, and medical billing information. This breach highlights the importance of robust cybersecurity measures in protecting sensitive patient information, as required under the Australian Privacy Act 1988 and the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme. Australian healthcare providers must ensure they have adequate measures in place to prevent and respond to data breaches, including implementing the ASD Essential Eight cybersecurity mitigation strategies. The settlement also underscores the potential financial and reputational consequences of a data breach, with the defendant establishing a $549,000 settlement fund to pay benefits to affected individuals. Australian healthcare providers should review their cybersecurity protocols and consider the potential risks and consequences of a data breach, including the need to notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner under the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme. By prioritising cybersecurity and complying with relevant regulations, including the Privacy Act 1988 and the Security of Critical Infrastructure Act, Australian healthcare providers can reduce the risk of a data breach and protect sensitive patient information. The Australian Cyber Security Centre's guidance on cybersecurity mitigation strategies should also be consulted to ensure that healthcare providers are taking a proactive approach to cybersecurity.

## What Australian Healthcare Organisations Should Do

- Implement the ASD Essential Eight cybersecurity mitigation strategies to prevent malware and ransomware attacks
- Regularly review and update cybersecurity protocols to ensure compliance with the Australian Privacy Act 1988 and the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme
- Conduct regular cybersecurity risk assessments and penetration testing to identify vulnerabilities and weaknesses in healthcare systems and networks
