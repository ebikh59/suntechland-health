A recent data breach at a Colorado-based substance use disorder treatment and behavioural health services provider has highlighted the risks of insider threats to healthcare organisations. NAS Recovery Solutions announced that certain workforce members had downloaded client data without authorisation, affecting up to 7,000 current and former clients. The breach, which was discovered on May 13, 2026, involved limited information such as names, dates of birth, and telephone numbers, but as the provider offers substance use disorder treatment, it could be inferred that the individuals were receiving such treatment. This incident serves as a reminder to Australian healthcare providers of the importance of ensuring the privacy and security of patient information, as required by the Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles. The Notifiable Data Breaches scheme also requires healthcare providers to notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner in the event of a data breach. In light of this incident, Australian healthcare providers should review their access controls and security measures to prevent similar insider breaches. The ASD Essential Eight and ACSC guidance provide valuable resources for healthcare providers to improve their cybersecurity posture and protect against data breaches. Furthermore, the Security of Critical Infrastructure Act 2018 also has implications for healthcare providers, particularly those that are considered critical infrastructure. By prioritising cybersecurity and implementing robust security measures, Australian healthcare providers can reduce the risk of data breaches and protect sensitive patient information. The incident at NAS Recovery Solutions also underscores the need for ongoing workforce training on patient privacy and cybersecurity best practices, as well as the importance of implementing sanctions against workforce members who compromise patient information. Australian healthcare providers should take note of these lessons and take proactive steps to prevent similar incidents from occurring in their own organisations.

## What Australian Healthcare Organisations Should Do

- Review and update access controls to ensure that workforce members only have access to necessary patient information
- Provide ongoing training to workforce members on patient privacy and cybersecurity best practices, including the importance of authorisation and authentication
- Implement additional security measures, such as multi-factor authentication and monitoring of system activity, to detect and prevent insider breaches
