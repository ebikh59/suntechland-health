A significant data breach has been disclosed by Centers Laboratory, a US-based diagnostic testing laboratory, affecting over 542,000 individuals. The breach occurred in August 2025, when suspicious activity was identified within the laboratory's computer systems. Following a forensic investigation, it was confirmed that an unauthorized third party had gained limited access to certain systems, resulting in the exfiltration of files containing patient data. The compromised information includes names, dates of birth, social security numbers, and medical information, among other sensitive details. Australian healthcare providers should take note of this incident, as it highlights the importance of robust cybersecurity measures in protecting sensitive patient information, as required by the Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles. The Notifiable Data Breaches scheme also mandates that eligible data breaches be notified to the affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner. In light of this incident, Australian healthcare providers should review their own cybersecurity protocols to ensure they are aligned with the ASD Essential Eight and ACSC guidance, and consider implementing additional measures to prevent similar breaches. The use of credit monitoring and identity theft protection services, as offered to the affected individuals in this breach, may also be an important consideration for Australian healthcare providers in the event of a data breach. Furthermore, the Security of Critical Infrastructure Act (SOCI Act) may also be relevant for certain healthcare providers, and they should ensure they are compliant with its requirements. By prioritising cybersecurity and taking proactive steps to protect patient data, Australian healthcare providers can reduce the risk of similar breaches occurring in the future.

## What Australian Healthcare Organisations Should Do

- Implement multi-factor authentication to prevent unauthorized access to systems and data
- Regularly update and patch software and systems to prevent exploitation of known vulnerabilities
- Conduct regular cybersecurity risk assessments and penetration testing to identify and address potential weaknesses
