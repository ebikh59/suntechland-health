A recent data breach at All About Women's Care in Colorado has compromised the data of up to 12,000 patients, highlighting the importance of robust cybersecurity measures in the healthcare sector. The breach, which involved unauthorized access to the practice's IT environment via an employee VPN account, resulted in the theft of sensitive patient information, including names, dates of birth, and clinical treatment details. This incident serves as a reminder to Australian healthcare providers of the need to prioritize data security and comply with the Privacy Act 1988 and Australian Privacy Principles. The Notifiable Data Breaches scheme requires entities to notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner in the event of a data breach, emphasizing the importance of having incident response plans in place. Australian healthcare providers should review their cybersecurity protocols, including access controls and network security, to prevent similar incidents. The ASD Essential Eight provides a framework for mitigating cyber threats, and ACSC guidance offers valuable resources for protecting sensitive patient information. Furthermore, the Security of Critical Infrastructure Act 2018 reinforces the need for healthcare providers to ensure the security and integrity of their systems and data. By prioritizing cybersecurity and complying with relevant regulations, Australian healthcare providers can minimize the risk of data breaches and protect sensitive patient information.

## What Australian Healthcare Organisations Should Do

- Implement multi-factor authentication to prevent unauthorized access to sensitive patient information
- Regularly review and update access controls, including VPN accounts, to prevent lateral movement in the event of a breach
- Conduct regular security audits and risk assessments to identify vulnerabilities and ensure compliance with the ASD Essential Eight and ACSC guidance
