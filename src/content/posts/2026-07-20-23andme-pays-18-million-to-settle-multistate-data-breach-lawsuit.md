A recent settlement in the United States has seen 23andMe, a genetic testing company, agree to pay $18 million to resolve allegations of cybersecurity failures that led to a data breach affecting 6.9 million customers. The breach, which occurred in October 2023, was the result of credential stuffing, where attackers used credentials obtained from other data breaches to gain access to 23andMe accounts. An investigation found that 23andMe lacked basic cybersecurity measures, including comparing users' passwords against blocklists of known breached passwords, requiring multifactor authentication, and having rate limiting or intrusion prevention measures in place. This settlement has implications for Australian healthcare providers, who must ensure they are complying with the Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles, particularly in relation to the protection of sensitive health information. The Notifiable Data Breaches scheme also requires entities to notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner in the event of a data breach. Australian healthcare providers should take note of the importance of implementing robust cybersecurity measures to prevent similar breaches, including following the ASD Essential Eight and ACSC guidance. The settlement also highlights the need for ongoing monitoring and review of security measures to prevent and detect breaches. Furthermore, the Security of Critical Infrastructure Act (SOCI Act) reminds healthcare providers of their obligations to protect their systems and data from cyber threats. By prioritising cybersecurity and complying with relevant regulations, Australian healthcare providers can reduce the risk of data breaches and protect sensitive patient information.

## What Australian Healthcare Organisations Should Do

- Implement multifactor authentication to prevent unauthorised access to systems and data
- Regularly compare users' passwords against blocklists of known breached passwords and update password policies accordingly
- Ensure robust logging and monitoring is in place to detect and respond to potential security incidents in a timely manner
