A recent data breach at Nissan has highlighted the importance of cybersecurity for Australian healthcare providers, particularly in relation to the use of third-party software. The breach, which affected current and former Nissan employees, was linked to a zero-day flaw in Oracle's PeopleSoft software, and resulted in the exposure of sensitive personal data, including national identification numbers and banking details. This incident serves as a reminder to Australian healthcare organisations to ensure that their systems and software are up-to-date and secure, in accordance with the Australian Privacy Principles and the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme. The use of vulnerable software can have serious consequences, including the compromise of sensitive patient data, and healthcare providers must take steps to mitigate these risks. In this case, the breach was attributed to a critical remote code execution bug in Oracle's PeopleSoft software, which was exploited by attackers. Australian healthcare providers should be aware of the potential risks associated with using third-party software and take steps to secure their systems, including implementing the ASD Essential Eight and following ACSC guidance. The incident also highlights the importance of monitoring for suspicious activity and having incident response plans in place. By taking these steps, Australian healthcare providers can help protect sensitive patient data and prevent similar breaches from occurring. The Privacy Act 1988 and the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme require healthcare providers to notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner in the event of a data breach, and to take steps to prevent future breaches. The SOCI Act also provides a framework for the secure handling of sensitive information. Australian healthcare providers should review their cybersecurity protocols and ensure that they are compliant with these regulations. The breach at Nissan is a reminder that cybersecurity is an ongoing process, and that healthcare providers must continually monitor and update their systems to prevent breaches.

## What Australian Healthcare Organisations Should Do

- Implement the ASD Essential Eight to mitigate the risk of cyber threats, including patching operating systems and applications, and restricting administrative privileges
- Follow ACSC guidance on securing systems and software, including the use of multi-factor authentication and regular security audits
- Develop and implement incident response plans to quickly respond to and contain data breaches, and to notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner as required under the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme
