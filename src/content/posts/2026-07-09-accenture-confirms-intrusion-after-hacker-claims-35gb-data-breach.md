A major security breach has been confirmed by Accenture, a global consulting firm, following a claim by a hacker that they had stolen 35GB of data from the company. The hacker, who goes by the handle '888', posted on a cybercrime forum that they had exfiltrated sensitive data including source code, RSA keys, and Azure Storage access keys, and were offering it for sale in exchange for the Monero digital currency. Accenture has stated that it has identified the source of the intrusion and remediated the incident, but has not confirmed whether the hacker's claims are genuine. The breach has significant implications for Australian healthcare providers, who must ensure that their own cybersecurity measures are robust to prevent similar incidents. Under the Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles, organisations have a duty to protect personal information from unauthorised access or disclosure. The Notifiable Data Breaches scheme also requires organisations to notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner in the event of a data breach. Australian healthcare providers should review their cybersecurity protocols to ensure they are aligned with the ASD Essential Eight and ACSC guidance to mitigate the risk of a similar breach occurring. The Security of Critical Infrastructure Act (SOCI Act) also imposes obligations on certain sectors, including healthcare, to implement robust cybersecurity measures. Given the potential consequences of a data breach, including reputational damage and financial losses, it is essential for Australian healthcare providers to prioritise cybersecurity and take proactive steps to prevent and respond to incidents.

## What Australian Healthcare Organisations Should Do

- Implement multi-factor authentication to prevent unauthorised access to systems and data
- Regularly update and patch software and systems to prevent exploitation of known vulnerabilities
- Conduct regular cybersecurity training and awareness programs for all staff to ensure they can identify and respond to potential threats
